summary

  This course examines how information is generated, how it is processed, and ultimately, useful information for those new to information statistics. This lecture focuses on the application of statistics in social science, medicine and quality control, including the understanding of various statistics that are frequently encountered in newspapers, TV, and everyday life around us.
